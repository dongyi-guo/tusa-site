# Submitting Event Applications

[][1]

This guide covers event applications, risk assessments, temporary stall permits, and liquor permits for your club activities.

**Before you start:** You need to create a draft event before submitting an event application. See [Creating Events] first.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## How to Access Event Applications[][2]

Event applications are submitted through the **Resources** tab in your club group.

### Getting There[][3]

Go to your club group

Click the **Resources** tab (admins only)

Click **New Application** to start a new submission

Or click **Ongoing/Completed** to check status of existing applications

**Direct URL:** `https://tusa-dev.its.utas.edu.au/clubs/clubs-index/{your-club}/resources/events/`

|-------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| **New Application**     | Start event/stall/liquor applications |
| **Ongoing/Completed**   | Track submitted applications          |
| **Medical Disclosures** | View attendee medical forms           |
| **Liability Waivers**   | View signed waivers                   |
| **Parental Consent**    | View consent forms for under-18s      |

## The Key Rule[][5]

**Almost every club activity needs an event application.**

The only exception is a regular committee/admin meeting of your club executives. Everything else – social events, workshops, activities, outings, stalls – needs to go through the system.

When in doubt, submit an application. It’s always better to check than to run an unapproved event.

## Alcohol at Your Event[][13]

Alcohol requires specific permits and additional risks.

### On Campus (Non-Licensed Venue)[][14]

For alcohol at campus venues (not a licensed bar):

Alcohol and Drug Use risks

Activities and Games (alcohol events)

**University Liquor Permit required**

May need **TAS Gaming Special Permit** (if alcohol included in ticket price)

### Off Campus – Licensed Venue[][15]

For events at pubs, bars, licensed venues:

Alcohol (Licensed Venue) risks

**No TUSA permit required** (venue has their own licence)

Still need event application

### Off Campus – Unlicensed Venue[][16]

For BYO events at parks, private venues, etc.:

Falls under Adventure category

**TAS Gaming Special Permit required**

### Alcohol Rules[][17]

**You must:**

Apply at least 2 weeks before

Have someone with RSA certificate present

Provide non-alcoholic alternatives

Have food available

Check IDs (anyone who looks under 25)

Not serve intoxicated persons

**You must not:**

Serve anyone under 18

Allow drinking games or rapid consumption

Make alcohol the focus of the event

### Junior Members and Alcohol Events[][18]

**Junior members (under 18) cannot attend events with alcohol.**

The website automatically restricts ticket purchases for juniors using membership tags. However, you should still:

**Check your attendance list** for any juniors before the event

**Verify at the door** – anyone who looks under 25 should show ID

**Turn away juniors** who somehow registered (refund their ticket)

If you have junior members in your club, clearly communicate which events they can and cannot attend.

### TUSA Hire Store[][22]

If you’re hiring equipment from TUSA (marquees, tables, chairs, BBQs, etc.), you need to place your hire order **before** submitting your event application.

**Quick summary:**

Place your order in the TUSA Hire Store (100% discount auto-applies)

Note your **order number**

Enter the order number in your event application (Field 197)

TUSA determines your final discount when reviewing your event

**Important:** Stock is reserved when you **checkout**, not while in cart. Don’t leave items sitting – complete the order to secure your booking.

**Emergency contacts** – Have parent/guardian contact details readily available

**Insurance** – Verify your insurance covers minors in adventure activities

## Temporary Stall Permits[][33]

If you’re setting up a stall or booth on campus, there are **two different processes** depending on location.

### Outdoor Stalls (TUSA Application)[][34]

For stalls in **outdoor areas** (courtyards, lawns, outdoor walkways), use the TUSA event application:

**When you need this:**

Market day participation (outdoor)

Promotional booths outside buildings

Outdoor bake sales or food stalls

O-Week tabling

Outdoor merchandise sales

**What’s required:**

Type of stall and purpose

Preferred outdoor location on campus

Setup and pack down times

Staff/volunteers managing it

Equipment needed (tables, chairs, power)

Waste/bin requirements

### Indoor Stalls (UTAS Campus Services)[][35]

For stalls in **indoor areas** (foyers, atriums, indoor common areas), you need to book through **UTAS Campus Services** – not TUSA.

**Process:**

Contact UTAS Campus Services

Complete their venue hire form: [Contract for Hire of University Venues]

Campus Services will allocate your indoor position

You may still need a TUSA event application for the activity itself (food, etc.)

**Why the difference?** UTAS manages indoor spaces directly. TUSA can approve outdoor activities but indoor venue bookings go through the university.

### If Your Stall Involves Food[][36]

Whether indoor or outdoor, food handling requirements apply – see the Food section above. You’ll need appropriate food safety documentation regardless of location.

## Submitting Your Application[][37]

### Step 1: Access the Form[][38]

Log in to the TUSA website

Go to your **Club Dashboard**

Navigate to **Risk & Events**

Click **New Application**

### Step 2: Event Details[][39]

Fill in:

Event name and description

Date and time (or date range for ongoing activities)

Location (on campus / off campus / address)

Expected attendance

Contact person for this event

### Step 3: Select What Applies[][40]

The form will ask about:

Food service (BBQ, catering, vendors)

Alcohol service

Infrastructure needs

Transport arrangements

Outdoor/adventure elements

Stall requirements

Based on your answers, relevant risk sections appear.

### Step 4: Review Risks[][41]

For each risk category:

Read the risks and control measures

Confirm you’ll implement the controls

Add any custom risks if needed

### Step 5: Attach Documents[][42]

You have two ways to attach documents:

**Option A: Use your Club Document Library**\
If you’ve previously uploaded documents to your club’s document library:

Look for the checkboxes in each section

Tick the boxes for documents you want to attach

Documents appear based on their category – if you don’t see a document, check it was uploaded with the correct category

**Option B: Upload New Documents**\
Upload new documents directly:

RSA certificates (for alcohol)

Food handling certificates

Insurance certificates (for external providers)

Venue booking confirmation

Any other relevant permits

**Tip:** Documents you upload here get added to your club’s document library for future use.

### Step 6: Save or Submit[][43]

**Save for Later:**

You can save your application and come back to it

Upload documents, gather information, then return to finish

Saved applications appear in your dashboard

**Submit:**

Review everything and submit when ready

You’ll receive confirmation and can track status in your dashboard

## Blanket Assessments[][44]

For regular, repeated activities (weekly training, monthly meetups), you can submit a **blanket assessment** covering a period of time rather than individual events.

Select “ongoing series” instead of “single event”

Set the date range (e.g., Semester 1)

The assessment covers all instances within that period

Still need to follow all control measures each time

## The Approval Process[][45]

### Timeline[][46]

| Type                      | Typical Processing |
|---------------------------|--------------------|
| Simple events             | 2-3 business days  |
| Events with alcohol       | 7-10 business days |
| Complex/adventure events  | 5-7 business days  |
| Large events (50+ people) | 5-7 business days  |

**Plan ahead!** Submit well before your event date.

### Possible Outcomes[][47]

| Status | What It Means |
|----|----|
| **Approved** | Good to go |
| **Approved with Conditions** | Approved, but you must meet specific requirements |
| **Returned for Amendment** | We need changes or more information |
| **Declined** | Not approved – see feedback for reasons |

### If Returned for Amendment[][48]

Check your email for feedback

Log in and view requested changes

Make amendments

Resubmit

## Frequently Asked Questions[][55]

### Do I really need an application for a small event?[][56]

Yes, unless it’s literally just a committee meeting. Even small social gatherings need an application – it protects you and your club.

### How far in advance should I apply?[][57]

Standard events: At least 1 week

Events with alcohol: At least 2 weeks

Large or complex events: At least 4 weeks

### What if my event details change?[][58]

Minor changes (small time adjustment) – contact TUSA to update.\
Major changes (new venue, different activities) – may need new application.

### Can I copy a previous application?[][59]

For similar recurring events, you may be able to reference previous applications. Contact TUSA for guidance.

### What if something goes wrong at my event?[][60]

Prioritise safety of attendees

Contact emergency services if needed (000)

Report incident to TUSA as soon as possible

Complete incident report within 24 hours
