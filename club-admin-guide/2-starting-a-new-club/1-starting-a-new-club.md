# Starting a New Club

[][1]

Want to start a new club at TUSA? Here’s how the process works.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## The Process Overview[][2]

    1. Submit Expression of Interest (EOI)
           ↓
    Meeting with TUSA Club Officer
           ↓
    Receive invitation to apply
           ↓
    Submit full affiliation application
           ↓
    Application reviewed and approved
           ↓
    Club created in system - you're affiliated!

**Important:** You cannot submit an affiliation application directly. You must first submit an EOI and meet with a Club Officer.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Step 1: Expression of Interest (EOI)[][3]

Before you can apply for club affiliation, you need to submit an Expression of Interest.

### What the EOI Asks[][4]

Your proposed club name

What the club is about

Why you want to start this club

Your contact details

### Where to Submit[][5]

Find the EOI form on the TUSA website under Clubs & Societies.

### What Happens Next[][6]

A TUSA Club Officer will contact you to arrange a meeting.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Step 2: Meeting with Club Officer[][7]

After submitting your EOI, you’ll meet with a TUSA Club Officer.

### Purpose of the Meeting[][8]

Discuss your club idea

Understand the requirements for affiliation

Get guidance on your constitution and documentation

Ask any questions you have

### What to Prepare[][9]

Your club concept clearly in mind

Any questions about requirements

Ideas about who might be on your executive committee

### After the Meeting[][10]

If your club idea is viable and meets TUSA requirements, you’ll be invited to submit a full affiliation application.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Step 3: Affiliation Application[][11]

Once invited to apply, you’ll need to prepare:

### Required Documents[][12]

| Document            | What It Is                                         |
| ------------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| **Constitution**    | Your club’s rules and governance structure         |
| **Meeting minutes** | Record of your founding meeting                    |
| **Member list**     | List of founding members (minimum number required) |

### Executive Committee[][13]

You’ll need to nominate your founding executive committee:

President (required)

Other committee roles as per your constitution

### Application Details[][14]

Club name and acronym

Club description and purpose

Contact details for all committee members

Student IDs for verification

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Step 4: What Happens After Approval[][15]

When your application is approved, the system automatically creates:

| Item                   | What You Get                               |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| **Club group**         | Your private group on the TUSA website     |
| **President account**  | Login for managing the club                |
| **Committee accounts** | Logins for your executive members          |
| **Club store**         | For selling memberships and merchandise    |
| **Startup grant**      | Automatic application for new club funding |

You’ll receive login details by email and can start setting up your club.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Timeline[][16]

| Stage                         | Typical Time             |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------ |
| EOI to meeting                | 1-2 weeks                |
| Meeting to application invite | Same day (if successful) |
| Application review            | 1-2 weeks                |
| **Total**                     | 2-4 weeks                |

Plan ahead if you want to be affiliated by a specific date.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Frequently Asked Questions[][17]

### Can I skip the EOI and just apply?[][18]

No. The EOI and meeting are required steps. This ensures clubs are viable and you understand the requirements before investing time in a full application.

### What if a similar club already exists?[][19]

The Club Officer will discuss this in your meeting. You may be able to join the existing club, or there may be room for a distinct club if the focus is different enough.

### How many members do I need?[][20]

There’s a minimum member requirement – the Club Officer will confirm the current number in your meeting.

### Do I need a constitution?[][21]

Yes. TUSA can provide a template constitution, or you can write your own following the required format.

### What if my application is rejected?[][22]

You’ll receive feedback on why. Common issues can often be fixed and resubmitted.
