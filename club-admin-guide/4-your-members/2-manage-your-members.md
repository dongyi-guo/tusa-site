# Managing Club Members

[][1]

This guide explains how to manage members in your club group – and importantly, when you should and shouldn’t use these tools.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## The Right Way to Add Members[][2]

**Members should join by purchasing membership from your store.**

When someone purchases a membership product:

They get the membership tag automatically

The tag grants access to your club group

They’re properly tracked in the system

They show up in your member exports

Their membership expires correctly

**This is the correct flow. Don’t bypass it.**

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Accessing Member Management[][3]

### Where to Find It[][4]

Go to your club group

Click **Manage** tab (admins only)

Click **Members**

Here you can see all members and their roles.

### What You Can Do[][5]

| Action         | How                    | When to Use                     |
| -------------- | ---------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| View members   | Just browse the list   | Anytime                         |
| Promote member | Click member → Promote | Giving admin access (see below) |
| Demote member  | Click member → Demote  | Removing admin access           |
| Remove member  | Click member → Remove  | Only in specific cases          |
| Add member     | Use “Add Members”      | **Almost never – see warning**  |

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## WARNING: Don’t Use “Add Members”[][6]

The “Add Members” function in Manage \> Members is **not the right way** to add club members.

### Why Not?[][7]

| What Happens                 | Problem            |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------ |
| User joins group             | ✓ Works            |
| User gets membership tag     | ✗ **NO**           |
| User shows in member exports | ✗ **Unreliable**   |
| User can access deals        | ✗ **May not work** |
| Membership expires correctly | ✗ **Won’t expire** |

**The member is in your group but doesn’t have the membership tag.** They’re not a “real” member in the system’s eyes.

### What to Do Instead[][8]

Tell them to purchase membership from your store – even if it’s free (\$0). This ensures they get the tag.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Removing Members[][17]

### When to Remove[][18]

Serious misconduct

Requested by the member

Duplicate accounts

### What Happens When You Remove[][19]

| Action            | What Happens                           |
| ----------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| Remove from group | User loses group access immediately    |
| Membership tag    | **Still has it** (doesn’t remove tags) |
| Can rejoin?       | Yes, if they still have the tag        |

**Removing from the group doesn’t remove their membership tag.** The tag expires on its set date.

### If You Need to Fully Remove Someone[][20]

Contact TUSA. They can:

Remove the membership tag

Block the user if needed

Handle serious issues appropriately

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Bulk Importing Members (CSV)[][21]

If you have a list of members to import (e.g., from a legacy system or external spreadsheet), you can use the **Member Import** tool.

### How to Access[][22]

Go to your club group

Look for the **Member Import** tab (if enabled for your club)

Only club admins/organisers can see this tab

### How It Works[][23]

**Select tags** – Choose which membership tags to apply (filtered to your club’s tags only)

**Upload CSV** – Drag and drop your member list

**Review** – Check the preview shows correct data

**Import** – Click Start Import

### CSV Format[][24]

Your spreadsheet needs these columns:

First Name

Family Name (or Last Name)

Email

Student Number

Column names are flexible – “first_name”, “First Name”, or “firstname” all work.

### What Happens[][25]

| If member…                    | Result                                     |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| **Already exists** (by email) | Gets your club’s tags added, joins group   |
| **New user**                  | Account created, tags applied, joins group |

**Note:** New users don’t receive a welcome email. They can use “Forgot Password” to set up their account.

### When to Use This[][26]

Importing members from a previous membership system

Adding a batch of members from an event sign-up sheet

Migrating from another club management tool

**For regular member sign-ups, always use the store** – this ensures proper tracking and tag expiry.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Summary: Do’s and Don’ts[][27]

### DO[][28]

✓ Have members purchase membership (even if free)

✓ Use Manage \> Members to promote committee to Organizer

✓ Demote people when they leave committee

✓ Check if someone is vendor staff elsewhere before promoting

✓ Use CSV import for bulk migration from legacy systems

### DON’T[][29]

✗ Use “Add Members” to add regular members

✗ Promote someone to Organizer without understanding the access it gives

✗ Expect someone to access two club stores as staff

✗ Remove members to “cancel” their membership (use tag expiry instead)

✗ Use CSV import instead of the store for regular sign-ups

## Junior Members (Under 18)[][30]

### What Is a Junior Member?[][31]

A junior member is any club member who is under 18 years old. The system automatically identifies juniors based on the birth date they provide during registration.

### Why Junior Members Matter[][32]

Junior members require special consideration for several reasons:

| Concern                  | Why It Matters                                               |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Parental consent**     | Clubs need parental permission before juniors can participate in activities |
| **Insurance**            | Some activities have different insurance requirements for minors |
| **Adventure activities** | Higher risk activities need explicit parental approval       |
| **Alcohol events**       | Juniors cannot attend or purchase tickets to events serving alcohol |

### Parental Consent Requirements[][33]

**Before a junior member can participate in club activities, you must have a signed parental consent form on file.**

**How it works:**

When a junior joins your club, they (or their parent) submit a parental consent form

These forms are stored in your club’s Resources tab (GravityView)

Before each event, check that all attending juniors have consent on file

No consent = no participation

### How to See Your Junior Members[][34]

Your club’s member list export shows junior members:

Go to your club group

Access the **Ready Reckoner** (member statistics display)

Junior members are listed by name in a separate section

Download the CSV for a complete list with “Is a Junior” column

### Before Events: The Junior Checklist[][35]

Before running an event where juniors may attend:

**Download your member list** or view the Ready Reckoner

**Identify juniors** on your attendance list

**Check consent forms** in Resources → Parental Consent

**Cross-reference** – every junior attending must have a consent form

**Turn away** any junior without consent on file (explain they need parent to submit form first)

### Adventure Clubs: Extra Considerations[][36]

If your club runs adventure activities (bushwalking, camping, water sports, etc.), junior participation requires:

Signed parental consent form for general membership

**Additional consent** may be needed for specific high-risk activities

Parents should understand the nature of activities

Consider having parent contact details readily available during activities

### Events with Alcohol[][37]

**Junior members cannot attend events where alcohol is served.**

The website automatically restricts alcohol event tickets using membership tags — juniors won’t be able to purchase tickets to these events. However, as an extra safeguard:

Check your attendance list for juniors before alcohol events

If a junior somehow registered, contact them to explain they cannot attend

Refund their ticket if applicable
