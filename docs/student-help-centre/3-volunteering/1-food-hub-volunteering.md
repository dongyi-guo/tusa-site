# Food Hub Volunteering

[][1]

The TUSA Food Hub provides free food to students in need. As a volunteer, you’ll help sort, pack, and distribute food while making a real difference in students’ lives.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## What is the Food Hub?[][2]

The Food Hub is TUSA’s food assistance program that:

- Provides **free groceries** to students experiencing food insecurity
- Operates at multiple **campus locations**
- Runs on **volunteer power** from students like you
- Sources food from donations and food rescue programs

## What Volunteers Do[][4]

### Typical Volunteer Tasks[][5]

| Task             | Description                             |
|------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| **Sorting**      | Checking and organising donated food    |
| **Packing**      | Preparing food parcels for distribution |
| **Distribution** | Helping students collect their food     |
| **Stocking**     | Restocking shelves and storage areas    |
| **Cleaning**     | Keeping the space clean and tidy        |

### What to Expect[][6]

A typical volunteer shift includes:

- Arriving a few minutes before your shift
- Checking in with the shift supervisor
- Getting assigned to tasks for the day
- Working alongside other volunteers
- Helping students who come to collect food
- Cleaning up at the end of your shift

## How to Become a Volunteer[][7]

### Step 1: Submit a Volunteer Application[][8]

1.  Log in to your TUSA account (or create one)
2.  Find the **Food Hub Volunteer Application** form
3.  Fill in your details and preferred location(s)
4.  Submit your application

**Note:** You cannot become a volunteer just by updating your profile – you must submit an application.

### Step 2: Wait for Approval[][9]

After submitting:

- Your application will be reviewed by the Food Hub team
- This may take a few days
- You’ll receive an email when your application is approved (or if we need more information)

### Step 3: Complete Onboarding[][10]

Once approved:

- You’ll receive information about next steps
- Complete any required training
- Learn about food safety basics
- Get familiar with Food Hub procedures

### Step 4: Book Your Shifts[][11]

After completing onboarding:

1.  Access the volunteer shift calendar (ShiftController)
2.  Browse available shifts at your chosen location(s)
3.  Book shifts that suit your schedule
4.  Receive confirmation of your bookings

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Shift Booking[][12]

### Finding Available Shifts[][13]

1.  Log in to the TUSA website
2.  Access the shift booking system
3.  Select your Food Hub location
4.  View the calendar of available shifts
5.  Click on a shift to see details

### Booking a Shift[][14]

1.  Find a shift that works for your schedule
2.  Click to book
3.  Confirm your booking
4.  Add it to your personal calendar

### Cancelling a Shift[][15]

If you can’t make a shift you’ve booked:

1.  Cancel as early as possible
2.  Go to your booked shifts
3.  Find the shift and cancel it
4.  The shift becomes available for others

**Please cancel at least 24 hours in advance** if possible. This helps us find replacement volunteers.

## Volunteer Expectations[][16]

### Commitment[][17]

- There’s **no minimum commitment** required
- Volunteer when you can
- Regular volunteers are appreciated but not required
- Even occasional shifts make a difference

### Reliability[][18]

- If you book a shift, please show up
- Arrive on time (or a few minutes early)
- If you can’t make it, cancel as soon as you know
- Communicate with supervisors if issues arise

### Conduct[][19]

- Treat all students with respect and dignity
- Maintain confidentiality about who uses the service
- Follow food safety guidelines
- Be a positive team member

## Frequently Asked Questions[][28]

### Do I need any experience?[][29]

No experience is needed! We provide all necessary training. Just bring a willingness to help.

### How long are volunteer shifts?[][30]

Shifts are typically 2-3 hours, but this varies. Check specific shift details when booking.

### Can I volunteer at multiple locations?[][31]

Yes! When registering, you can select multiple preferred locations.

### What should I wear?[][32]

Wear comfortable, closed-toe shoes and clothes that can get a bit dirty. Avoid loose jewellery.

### Is there a minimum age to volunteer?[][33]

Volunteers should be 16 or older. Under-18s may need parental consent.

### Can I volunteer during holidays?[][34]

Food Hub hours may be reduced during semester breaks. Check the calendar for available shifts.

### What if I’m running late for my shift?[][35]

Let us know as soon as possible. Contact the Food Hub supervisor or use the notification system.

### Can volunteering count toward course requirements?[][36]

Some courses accept community service hours. Check with your faculty about requirements and documentation.

### Do I get free food for volunteering?[][37]

Volunteers don’t automatically receive food, but if you’re experiencing food insecurity, you’re welcome to use the Food Hub like any other student.

## Accessing the Food Hub as a Student[][38]

### If You Need Food Assistance[][39]

The Food Hub is available to any student experiencing food insecurity:

- No judgement, no proof required
- Simply visit during operating hours
- Collect available groceries
- Return as often as you need

### What’s Available[][40]

Food Hub stock varies but typically includes:

- Fresh fruit and vegetables
- Pantry staples (rice, pasta, etc.)
- Bread and bakery items
- Canned goods
- Personal care items (when available)
